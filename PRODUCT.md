# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Import/export businesses, freight managers, procurement teams, and supply chain directors across global markets. They are time-sensitive professionals who need a reliable, capable logistics partner they can trust completely. They arrive with specific cargo needs — often complex multi-modal shipments — and need confidence in both the provider's capabilities and local knowledge.

## Product Purpose

Hill & Delamain is a global freight forwarding company with 20+ years of experience, 30 offices across 11 countries, and 100+ staff. The website communicates capability, reach, and reliability to prospective clients. It exists to convert business prospects into enquiries and reinforce trust with existing clients. Success = a visitor confidently reaching out to discuss their freight needs.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, global, dependable. "Precision. Reach. Care."

Tone: confident without boasting, expert without being distant, personal without being casual. The kind of freight partner that picks up the phone and already knows the answer.

## Anti-references

- Cheap courier/parcel sites (Parcelforce, Hermes) — too consumer, too transactional
- Overly corporate/cold enterprise portals (generic SAP-looking dashboards)
- Startup-feel logistics brands trying to look like fintech (Flexport landing page gimmicks)
- Heavy use of stock photography of smiling warehouse people

## Design Principles

1. **Show scale, earn trust** — numbers and geography speak louder than claims. Put the stats front and centre.
2. **One company, all capabilities** — the brand promise is breadth without fragmentation. Every section reinforces completeness.
3. **Authority through restraint** — strong typography and a tight colour palette. Not flashy; exact.
4. **Local knowledge, global backbone** — lead with local expertise claims before the global network. Clients care about their shipment, not the map.
5. **Precision in every detail** — consistent spacing, sharp alignment, zero ambiguity in copy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All text must hit ≥4.5:1 contrast. Reduced-motion alternative for all animations. Keyboard-navigable navigation with visible focus states.
